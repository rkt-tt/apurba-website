Bio Sketch

Prof. Apurba Das is a Professor in the Department of Textile and Fibre Engineering. He joined IIT Delhi in 2002 after more than a decade of experience in the textile industry and research organizations.

His research interests span a wide range of areas including clothing comfort, technical textiles, nonwovens, smart compression bandages, electro-textiles, thermo-acoustic textiles and instrumentation. He has successfully completed multiple research and consultancy projects funded by government agencies and industries and holds several granted and filed patents. He has also developed numerous instruments for the characterization of textile materials many of which have been transferred to industry.

Prof. Das received the Teaching Excellence Award in 2014 for his dedication to teaching. He has developed a variety of courses at IIT Delhi like Science of Clothing Comfort, Technical Textiles, and Evaluation of Textile Materials. In addition, he has contributed several courses to NPTEL and Swayamprabha which have been widely appreciated by students and professionals. He has guided many Ph.D., MTech., and B.Tech. students and continues to mentor scholars in their academic careers.

He has extensive publications in leading journals and conferences and is the author and editor of several books. Along with being the reviewer, he is the editorial board member of several high impact journals.

Prof. Das has participated in international research collaborations with institutions in Hungary, Portugal, Poland, Slovenia, Germany, and Italy. He is a member of the Research Advisory Committee of NITRA and serves on the Boards of Studies of several institutions in the country.

    Multidirectional thermal protective performance testing instrument

    An instrument is designed and developed for measuring heat transfer through fabrics and skin burn subjected to flame/fire and radiant heat exposures under various orientations.

    Water Purification System and Antibacterial Cartridge system

    The nonwoven fabric-based filtration system was developed to provide safe drinking water in rural areas. It uses a two-stage process to remove solids, bacteria, and heavy metals. The system is eco-friendly, low-cost, and operates without electricity, ensuring sustainability and ease of use.

    DST: (Purchased with grant from DST)

    Contact-heat TPP Tester

    The contact heat transmission tester evaluates how protective clothing, gloves and other related materials transmit heat when in contact with hot surfaces. It measures contact temperature and threshold time using a heated cylinder and calorimeter under controlled force. The system complies with EN 702 and ISO 12127-1 standards.

    NTTM: (Purchased with grant from NTTM)

    Microclimate Simulator Cum Moisture Vapour Transmission Tester (MS- MVTT)C

    The MS-MVTT, developed at IIT Delhi, measures changes in microclimate temperature and humidity over time. It simulates the human body by maintaining water vapor at 37°C, which passes through a porous membrane and fabric specimen. Sensors record humidity and temperature variations to evaluate fabric moisture vapor permeability under both still and windy conditions.

    NTTM/DST: DRDO (Replace this Muffle Furnace Timax 12000 C)

    Freeze Dryer

    Freeze dryer for aerogel synthesis is a widely employed technique due to its ability to create aerogels with highly desirable characteristics, including high porosity, structural integrity, and purity.

    NTTM/DST: DRDO

    High-Temperature Muffle Furnace

    The Carbolite Jero Muffle Furnace is a high-temperature unit capable of reaching 1200 °C, used for polymerization and thermal treatment of polymer materials. It ensures uniform heating, precise temperature control, and energy-efficient performance for research and material development.

    NTTM: (Purchased with grant from NTTM)

    Tomodynamometer

    The TDM 100 Tomodynamometer (Origin Quebec, Canada) accurately measures the linear cut resistance of protective materials. It determines the force required for a blade to cut through a sample in one controlled motion. The device complies with international standards such as ISO 13997, ASTM F2992, EN 388, and ANSI/ISEA 105.

    NTTM: (Purchased with grant from NTTM)

    Supercritical CO2

    Dryer will be used to dry the aerogel to obtain desirable flexible and robust structure, simplifying the synthesis process by eliminating the need for multiplesolvents and drying steps.

    NTTM/DST: DRDO

    Thermal Imaging Camera

    FLUKE Ti450 PRO IR thermal imaging camera provides sharp and accurate temperature profile of the field of view. The available compatible software connects wirelessly and provides wide range of analysis and graph formation within 1D or 2D analysis.

    NTTM: (Purchased with grant from NTTM)

    Impedance Tube

    Measurements:- Sound absorption coefficient (SAC)
    - Sound transmission loss (STL)
    - Test Range:- 63 Hz - 6300 Hz

    NTTM: (Purchased with grant from NTTM)

    Radient TPP Tester

    The Radiative Protective Tester evaluates the thermal resistance of fabrics under radiant heat exposure. It uses five quartz bulbs with a voltage regulator to generate controlled heat flux levels (20–40 kW/m²). A copper calorimeter measures heat transmission, enabling quantitative assessment of radiant heat protection.

    NTTM/DST: In House Developed

    Stab resistant tester

    The Stab Resistant Tester by Instron, USA, evaluates a material’s resistance to sharp, pointed weapons. Designed per NIJ Standard 0115.00, it is essential for testing and validating the safety performance of body armor used by high-risk personnel.
